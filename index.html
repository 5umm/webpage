What is Computer Engineering

BSCPE typically stands for Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering. It is an undergraduate degree program that focuses on the study of computer systems, hardware, software, and the integration of both to design and develop various computing solutions. Students pursuing a BSCPE degree typically learn about computer architecture, digital electronics, programming languages, embedded systems, and other related topics. This degree equips graduates with the knowledge and skills needed for careers in fields such as computer engineering, software development

What We Do?

BS CpE students are encouraged to get involved in a variety of activities during their studies. This will help them to develop their skills and knowledge, gain real-world experience, and network with other students and professionals.

Courses

BS CpE students take the curriculum that covers the fundamentals of computer engineering,They also take courses in specialized areas such as software engineering, embedded systems, and computer networks.

Laboratory Works

BS CpE students spend a significant amount of time in the laboratory, They may work on projects such as designing and building electronic circuits, programming microcontrollers, and developing software applications.

Research Project

BS CpE students complete a reserch project, which is a major design or research project that demonstrates their skills and knowledge. and they often involve working on real-world problems with industry partners.

Mission

The Department of Computer Engineering declares its role to produce competitive engineers who are knowledgeable in the fundamental areas of digital and computer system design, software development, and modeling of physical systems.

Vision

The Department of Computer Engineering will impact the information age as a national leader in computing, research, and education.

Program Educational Objectives

Have pursued advancement towards becoming globally competitive leaders in their chosen field of practice

Be a contributor to the development of a progressive society, guided by the UE core values of Excellence, Integrity, Professionalism, Teamwork, Commitment, Transparency, Accountability and Social Responsibility.

Program Outcomes

Apply knowledge of mathematics, science and engineering.

Design and conduct experiments, as well as to analyze and interpret data.

Design a system, component or processes to meet desired needs within realistic constraints.
